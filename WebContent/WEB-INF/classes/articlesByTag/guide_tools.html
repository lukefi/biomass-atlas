Region selection tools
Free outlining
Draw the region on the map. The application calculates the biomass. Start drawing by clicking on the map. Draw a line with the mouse, and click at every corner of the area. Stop drawing by double-clicking.

Predefined region
The biomass data can be retrieved for pre-determined regions. The types of region to choose from are: municipalities, provinces, Centres for Economic Development, Transport and the Environment (ELY-keskus), catchment areas or postcode regions. After choosing the type of region, choose the desired areas by clicking the map with the mouse. "Choose all" function chooses all regions at once. After this you can remove regions by clicking on them on the map.

The centre of a circle or road network
Choose first the central point of the desired area on the map, and then write in the opening window the distance from the central point. The biomass within the created circle will be calculated. The distance from the central point can be along the beeline or along the road network.

Move between maps (arrow tool icons) (Top of page)
Move to default view, previous view or the next view. Click corresponding arrow tool. Default view is the map that is open when you enter the map service. Moving to the default view zeros your search and you have to make the selections again. You can move to default view also by clicking the arrow in the centre of the panning tool.

Zoom in or zoom out the map (Top of page)
Click the tool "Zoom in map" and zoom in the map with one of the following ways:
  1. Mark out with the mouse the area that you want to be shown in the map.
  2. Double-click a point on the map. The map will be zoomed in and centered to the selected point.
Choose a suitable scale with one of the following ways:
  1. Change scale with the scaling tool (right upper area of the map).
  2. Change scale with the keyboard + and - keys.
    3. The scale bar of the map is shown at the lower left corner of the map.

Move the map (Top of page)
Move the map in one of the following ways:
  1. Click the panning arrows in the upper right area of the map.
  2. Use the arrow keys of your keyboard.
  3. Click the tool "Move map" and drag the map with the mouse.
    4. You can see the coordinates of the map view centre and the coordinate system information below the scaling tool in the upper right area of the map.

Measure the distance (Top of page)
Choose the tool "Measure the distance" and measure the distance:
  1. Click the starting point and the turning points.
  2. Read the distance in metres in the opening window.
  3. Double-click the end point to stop the measurement.

Measure the area (Top of page)
Choose the tool "Measure the area" and measure the area:
  1. Draw the border line of the area by clicking at each corner of the area.
  2. Read the area in square metres in the opening window.
  3. To stop measuring, double-click at the last corner.

Save the map view and the biomass selections (Top of page)
Save the map view and the selections you made in the "Biomasses" menu in order to use them later:
  1. Click the tool "Save the map view and the biomass selections".
  2. Give the view a name and description. Name is compulsory.
  3. Click "Save".
Saved map views and biomass selections are found in the "My data" menu on the "Map views" -interleaf. In the interleaf you can:
  1. Open a saved map view and biomass selections
  2. Edit a map view and biomass selections
  3. Delete a map view and biomass selections
    4. Only registered users can save map views and biomass selections.

Create a link to map view and biomass selections (Top of page)
Create a link to map view and biomass selections and forward it:
  1. Choose the tool "Link to map view".
  2. Copy the link from the opening window using keys Ctrl-C.
  3. Paste the link in e.g. an e-mail using keys Ctrl-V.

Marking of a map (Top of page)
Mark a point in the map:
  1. Choose the tool "Mark the map".
  2. Click a point of interest on the map.
  3. Define the design, size and color of the marking symbol.
  4. Give the text that will be shown beside the symbol.
  5. Click the "Save" handle.
    6. You can delete all map markings by choosing the tool "Mark the map" and clicking on the option "Delete all map markings".
