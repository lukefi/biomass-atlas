Biomasses have been grouped in the "Biomasses" menu according to "Potential" and "Origin".

"Potential" refers here to different stages of biomass production and usage. Land use forms the basis for the biomass production. Total production-interleaf gives the total biomass production on a certain land area. Side stream is the share of the biomass, which is left over after the primary usage of the biomass.

"Origin" describes, where the biomass originates from: forest, agriculture, communities or companies.

More detailed descriptions of the fractions (metadata) are found in the menu "Selected layers", under the info icon of each fraction.

Every biomass map layer forms its own dataset. User selections, whether they are selections of other biomasses or background maps, don't affect the pre-calculated biomass potentials. Only the area definition tools affect the amount of biomasses.


You can delete all selections using the button at the top right hand corner of the "Biomasses" menu.
